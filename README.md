# 🧠 Brain vs. Phone — The Happiness Lab

An interactive web app for kids (ages ~10–16) and their families that teaches:

- **How apps hook the brain** — dopamine, variable rewards, infinite scroll, streaks, notifications (inspired by *The Social Dilemma*, *Screened Out*, *Childhood 2.0*)
- **Why attention is valuable** — "if it's free, you're the product" (*The Great Hack*)
- **What actually builds happiness** — the three "macronutrients" (enjoyment, satisfaction, meaning), shiny traps vs. real paths, from Dr. Arthur Brooks (FoundMyFitness podcast #110, *"How To Build Lasting Happiness"*)
- **Tech isn't the villain** — tool vs. trap thinking, good uses of technology
- **Doing, not just reading** — 7 real-world happiness experiments, a family movie-night guide with discussion questions, and a printable two-sided family screen pledge (grown-ups promise things too!)

## The 6 missions

1. 🎣 **The Hook** — a "mystery feed" slot-machine simulator + a flip-card deck of design tricks and power moves
2. ⏳ **Your Time Machine** — an attention calculator (minutes/day → days/year → years of your life)
3. 😊 **The Happiness Formula** — build your perfect Saturday and watch the three happiness nutrients fill up
4. 🎯 **Myth or Fact?** — a 10-question quiz with explanations
5. ⚖️ **Tool or Trap?** — sort 10 real-life tech situations
6. 🔥 **Happiness Experiments** — 7 checkable real-world challenges

Finish all 6 to unlock a printable **Brain Master certificate**. Progress is saved locally in the browser.

## Running it

It's a fully static site — no build step, no dependencies. Open `index.html` in a browser, or serve the folder with any static server. Deploys as-is to Vercel/Netlify/GitHub Pages.

## Files

- `index.html` — structure and content
- `styles.css` — styling (mobile-first)
- `app.js` — all interactivity and educational content data
